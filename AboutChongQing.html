Brief Introduction



Overall Introduction:

Chongqing is one of China's municipalities with a population of 32.1334 Million.It covers an area of 82,400 square kilometers. In ancient times, Chongqing was called "Yushui" or "Yu" for short. Once a wartime capital, it became one of China's four major municipalities in 1997. Located in inland southwest China, the upper reaches of the Yangtze River region. It is a provincial-level administrative region and municipality directly under the Central Government of the People's Republic of China. It is adjacent to Hubei and Hunan in the east, Guizhou in the south, Sichuan in the west and Shaanxi in the north. It covers an area of 82,400 square kilometers and has jurisdiction over 38 districts and counties (26 districts, 8 counties and 4 autonomous counties). The majority of the population is Han, and the main ethnic minorities are Tujia and Miao. Chongqing is surrounded by mountains and built on mountains. Its landform is dominated by hills and mountains, among which mountains account for 76%. The Yangtze River runs through the whole territory, with a flow of 691 kilometers, and Jialing River, WuJiang River and other rivers. Because Chongqing is located at the edge of the basin and the confluence of two rivers, it is foggy all year round, so it is also called "Mountain City" and "fog capital".

Climate:

The climate of Chongqing is very special, belonging to the subtropical monsoon humid climate, hot summer and warm winter, humid more overcast, high temperature, long rainy season, less frost and snow, more cloudy days, high humidity. Warm in winter and hot in summer, due to depressed terrain and sultry climate, it has become one of the three "furnaces" of the Yangtze River.
