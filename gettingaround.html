What is eighteen stairs?

Shibati is not unfamiliar to locals. As a corridor connecting the upper and lower half of the city, Shibati has been one of Chongqing's best-preserved connecting passages for nearly a thousand years, and it once occupied a very important position in history. Eighteen Stairs starts from Houci Street in the south to Jiaochangkou in the north. Eighteen stairs is not only eighteen stone stairs.

In the Ming and Qing Dynasties, it was located on a long slope, where residents built their homes against the mountains. It is said that there was a local well, and the nearby residents ate the water from the well. The well was divided into 18 layers of stone stairs because it was more than 200 years away from the residents' homes, so it was named eighteen Ladder.

Eighteen Ladder after five years of transformation and upgrading, the level of appearance has also risen to a great extent. At the same time, while preserving the traditional mountain city style, wooden shops are arranged in the street. In the evening, when the lights are bright, the whole Shibaiti becomes a night market of lights, which echoes with the lights of the surrounding families, showing a school of fireworks, and the vision and soul are cured by this scene.

In Shibati, you can visit the Shancheng Memory Museum, which displays a large number of rare books and videos of old Chongqing, and is a must for learning about the history of Chongqing. The Shibati Grand Tunnel Site Memorial Hall is a heavy historical memory for Chongqing people. You can go here to feel the "memory of Chongqing in the bomb shelter". Through the iron gate, prying into the Chongqing eighty or ninety years ago experienced pain, feel those "more and more strong" years of war.

You can go to Zhu Bingren Art Museum and experience the magnificent copper carving art in the 1000-square-meter immersive copper art space. Every brick, tile, table and chair are all made of copper, showing the strong characteristics of Hui style architecture of Ming and Qing Dynasties.

Jiefangbei

Monument of the Victory of the Anti-Japanese War

The People's Liberation Monument, also known as the "Monument of the Victory of the Anti-Japanese War", or "Monument of Liberation" for short, is located in the center of the commercial pedestrian street of Jiefangbei, Yuzhong District, Chongqing. It is a spiritual symbol of the victory of the Anti-Japanese War and a monument to commemorate the victory of the Chinese nation in the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression. The monument to the Liberation of the People is facing north to the east. It is an eight-sided column with a helmet and reinforced concrete structure, with a height of 27.5 meters and a side length of 2.55 meters. In September 2000, it became the first batch of municipal cultural relic protection units in Chongqing. In May 2013, it was listed in the "seventh batch of National Key Cultural Relics Protection Units" by The State Council. In September 2016, it was selected into the "First batch of Chinese 20th Century Architectural Heritage" list.

With a height of 24 meters and a diameter of 4 meters, the monument is an eight-sided tower, divided into the monument platform, the tablet seat, the body and the observation platform. The monument is 20 meters in diameter and 1.6 meters in height. There are flower beds on the steps.

The tablet is composed of 8 bluestone bollard, with 8 stele faces, using the superior gorge stone produced in Beibei. The stone tablets are engraved on the outside of the tablet, respectively: the Order of the Executive Yuan of the Nationalist Government on September 6, 1940, naming Chongqing as the auxiliary capital; the Inscription on the Victory of the Anti-Japanese War written by Wu Dingchang, the civil officer of the Nationalist Government; and the article describing Chongqing's contribution to the Anti-Japanese War written by Zhang Qun.

The front of the monument faces the direction of Minzu Road. It is inscribed with seven characters: "Monument of Victory of the Anti-Japanese War", which was written by Zhang Dulun, then mayor of Chongqing.

The inside of the monument is circular, with cantilever spiral ladder 140 steps, helicopter to the top of the observatory. Along the winding stairs is the Victory corridor of the Anti-Japanese War, on which are hung pictures of anti-Japanese heroes, great achievements and Japan's surrender. Below are embedded commemorative stone tablets presented by various provinces and cities, stone tablets presented by celebrities, etc. The translation of the scroll presented by the American President Roosevelt to Chongqing in 1943 is also inscribed on the wall of the victory corridor.

The observatory is 4.5 meters in diameter and can accommodate 20 visitors. It is equipped with anemometer, anemometer, north pointer and other measuring instruments on the top of the observatory. An alarm bell is hung on the top for gatherings and alarm of the whole city. The top of the monument is surrounded by eight mercury sun lamps, which shine brightly at night.

Bayu stilted Buildings

The mountain city of Chongqing, because of the particularity of its geographical location, has some very special buildings that are worthy of your visit. Hongyadong is one such scenic spot. Hongya Cave stands by the Jialing River, in the form of a three-dimensional air pedestrian street. It's just a little more commercial these days. On the first floor, there is a visitor service center and Hongyadong official gift House. The second floor is Hongyadong folk characteristic business hall, small goods and clothing; There are some small shops on the third floor and an outdoor deck. The platform becomes a viewing platform with a clear view of the Jialing River and Qiansimen Bridge. white elephant street

On the side of the wall there was a huge stone, like an elephant, named White Elephant

Chongqing's net celebrity attractions in the end how many? Objectively speaking, you can't count on ten fingers. If you have enough time at your fingertips, you can live in this city for at least a week, eating and drinking, walking through the streets and feeling the fireworks of the mountain city.

The most popular Internet attractions in Chongqing are in Yuzhong District, the mother city. Compared with Hongya Cave, Chaotianmen and other traditional scenic spots, the western style of White Elephant Street gives people a bright feeling.

White Elephant Street, located between Wanglongmen and Taipingmen in Yuzhong District of Chongqing, was a cultural center as early as the Southern Song Dynasty. During the period of Chunyou in the Southern Song Dynasty, Yu Wangjie, the governor of Chongqing, opened a recruitment hall here to offer advice on the anti-Mongolia plan. What many people don't know is that the first rich telegraph office in Chongqing opened on Baixiang Street. When Chongqing opened its port in 1891, there were many foreign banks and financial institutions along White Elephant Street, comparable in size and influence to the Bund of Shanghai and the Thirteen Banks of Guangzhou.

At that time, White Elephant Street was a "celebrity district" in Chongqing. The hosts and writers of different newspapers lived here, and Chen Yi and Lao She also left their footprints. Many old buildings from the early days of the port are preserved here, which combine Chinese and Western characteristics in style and are a kind of eclectic architecture. In addition, Baixiang Street also has some typical stilted houses and Baroque style houses. Even the private Xinghua Primary School, which was built in 1953, is eclectic in style. The former Chongqing Introspect School, built in 1930, used to be the Shanxi Merchants Hall earlier. The so-called Shanxi Business Hall is simply the headquarters of Shanxi Chamber of Commerce. From this perspective, it can be seen that Chongqing had high commercial development potential long ago. The city's biggest advantage is to guard the intersection of Yangtze River and Jialing River.

White Elephant Street is composed of several streets crisscrossing, and the buildings are mostly made of black brick as the main building material. Compared to other Internet celebrity neighborhoods, Baixiang Street is relatively quiet, and its stylish architecture makes it a haven for photography lovers, as well as newlyweds and tone-sensitive bloggers. Relatively speaking, White Elephant Street is more attractive to female tourists than male tourists, because female tourists prefer to take portraits, while male tourists stroll around more. In White Elephant Street, it is easy to take photos of the retro style and literary style, wearing fashionable clothes, Qing Dynasty clothes and clothes of the early Republic of China, and the scenery is very good.
